Lewis B. "Chesty" Puller

The Marines Marine

Timeline of Lewis B. Puller:

June 26, 1898 - Lewis Burwell Puller is born.

1936 - After earning his first Navy Cross for his leadership against a rebellion in Nicaragua, Lewis B. "Chesty" Puller returns to the United States to teach at the Marine Officers Basic School.

1940 - Lewis B. "Chesty" Puller returns to China as the executive officer of the 2nd Battalion of the 4th Marine Regiment in Shanghai.

1942 - Lewis B. "Chesty" Puller and his troops are sent to Guadalcanal during World War II.

1944 - Lewis B. "Chesty" Puller earns his fourth Navy Cross at Cape Gloucester in New Guinea when he braves enemy fire to inspire his men during a Japanese counterattack.

1944 - Lewis B. "Chesty" Puller returns to the United States to train recruits.

1950 - During the Korean War, Lewis B. "Chesty" Puller earns his fifth Navy Cross at the Chosin Reservoir.

1951 - Lewis B. "Chesty" Puller is promoted to brigadier general of the U.S. Marine Corps.

1953 - Lewis B. "Chesty" Puller is promoted to major general of the U.S. Marine Corps.

1955 - Lewis B. "Chesty" Puller is forced to retire from the U.S. Marine Corps because of ill health. He would later request to return to service only to be denied.

October 11, 1971 - Lewis B. "Chesty" Puller dies in Hampton.
